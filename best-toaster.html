15 Best Toasters 2022 | The Sun UK

THE best toaster will brown your bread just the way you like it and is versatile enough to cook other foods, too.

We've rounded up our pick of the best models, explained what to look for when choosing the right toaster for you, and answered some frequently asked questions.

What to look for in a toaster

Size

When it comes to selecting your chosen-toaster, the first thing to consider is size. Most designs are two-slice options, but if you have a big family then you might want to upgrade to a four-slice model.

If you plan to cook things like bagels or toasties, look for an option with variable width and extra wide slots. The wider slots mean that bigger items can fit in, and metal frames that come together to hold your item in place mean that, even if it’s just a thin piece of toast, it will be in the right position to be browned evenly.

Value

A decent toaster should have a crumb tray, and buttons for reheat, defrost and cancel — although you might be happy to sacrifice one or two of these functions for a cheaper price.

Having a range of different shade settings can be important as well. The more numbers there are, the more likely you are to find a shade to suit you.

Think of what you need and the best price of a toaster which can provide these things for you.

Design

Of course, there's no point splashing out on a fabulous toaster that doesn't suit your colour-scheme and makes you want to hide it in the cupboard.

Think of your colour scheme and how much space you have to place your gadget.

What is the best toaster?

It might be easy to immediately think of household names like KitchenAid, Smeg and De'Longhi when deciding on the best toaster around, but in reality, it comes down to your personal needs.

Some designs are great but they might not go well with your interior aesthetic, whereas others are basic but could be the finishing touch to your dream kitchen.

Decide on your budget, needs and colour-scheme first of all, then you can find the perfect one for you.

If you're always reaching for bagels or thick bread from your local bakery (yum) then you're going to need one that has extra thick slots to save slices getting stuck inside.

Sound a little more complicated than you thought? Don't worry, we've found the best toasters on the market today, whatever you're looking for.

Where to buy a toaster

Toasters are widely available from high street shops and even supermarkets.

You can also find a huge selection online, including at Amazon, Argos, Currys, John Lewis and Wilko.

How much are toasters?

Toasters can start from less than £10 and go up to hundreds of pounds.

Price isn’t necessarily an indicator of quality though - having all the right features is much more important.

What are the best toaster brands?

Like other kitchen appliances, the best toaster for you will depend on your preferences and the features you’re looking for so there’s no single brand that’s the best as such.

That said, a longer manufacturer’s guarantee is a good sign that the toaster is more reliable and will last longer - but even this could vary between models from the same brand.

You could however start off with some well known brands such as Bosch, Breville and Russell Hobbs.

How to clean a toaster

Before you do anything, make sure your toaster is unplugged - this is very important.

Most toasters now have a crumb tray and you can simply slide this out and empty it in the bin. It’s a good idea to give this a wash too, if you haven’t for a while. Washing up liquid followed by a rinse is fine — just make sure it’s dry before you put it back at the end of the clean.

Next, turn your toaster upside down over a bin or the sink and give it a good shake so any loose crumbs will fall out. Use a sturdy pastry brush to remove any crumbs lodged in the corners. And if you have any stuck on bits of food, use the handle of a wooden spoon to scrape it off and scrub away any debris with a clean toothbrush.

For the outside, simply give it a wipe down with a dampened microfiber cloth. You can dab on a bit of washing up liquid if it’s greasy but make sure none of it gets inside the toaster. Wipe down with a damp cloth afterward.

Once your toaster is clean, just slot the crumb tray back in and you’re ready to use it again.
