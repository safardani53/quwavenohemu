Herbert W. Armstrong was an American Christian missionary who founded God's Church worldwide. As an early pioneer of radio and television evangelization, Armstrong preached what he claimed to be the complete combination of doctrines throughout the Bible, in the light of the covenant scriptures. New, which he claims is the truly restored gospel. These theories and doctrines were called Armstrongism by those who disobeyed. Armstrong's teachings required compliance with certain parts of the Mosaic law, including the seventh Sabbath, the prohibitions on fasting, and the law of the covenant "on holy days." He also claims that behind contemporary world events are many biblical prophecies, interpreted in the light of English Israel. As the founder and head of the Ambassador International Cultural Foundation, Armstrong and his advisers met with heads of government from a variety of countries, whom he considered himself to be. an "ambassador without a portfolio of world peace".
