Naim Sliti- All About The Professional Player From The Tunisia National Team

Naim Sliti is a professional footballer who now plays for Al-Ettifaq as an attacking midfielder. He was born in France to Tunisian parents and now plays for the national team of Tunisia.

So far in his career

Sliti made his professional debut with CS Sedan after a youth career with Marseille. He played for the club for two years, appearing in 53 games between Sedan A and Sedan B and scoring three goals. He subsequently moved on to Paris FC, where he scored nine goals in 18 games. In 2014, he joined Red Star.

He was loaned out to Lille while at Red Star before signing a permanent deal with the club in 2017. He joined Dijon on loan from Lille and subsequently returned to Dijon for a season before joining Al-Ettifaq in 2019. He made his international debut for Tunisia in a 2017 Africa Cup of Nations qualifier, scoring his first goal in a 3-0 victory against Djibouti.

He has wanted to participate in the World Cup Since he began playing football

Sliti will compete for Tunisia in the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar for the second time. Playing in the World Cup is a dream come true for Sliti, who claims he has wanted to participate in the World Cup since he first began playing football. According to him, he began playing football shortly after France won the 1988 World Cup with two goals from Zinedine Zidane in the final.

That’s what prompted him to start playing. Sliti said that it is an honor for him to play for Tunisia, that he gives his best on every occasion to wear the Carthaginian eagles’ shirt, and that he will play at the World Cup for a whole people.

The most significant effect on his career

His parents have had the most effect on his career and have been there for him throughout his football career. He says all he can do is thank them for all they’ve done for him. Aside from that, he had instructors, particularly at Sedan, who encouraged him to play the game at the best level possible before becoming professional.

He selected the Saudi League over La Liga for religious considerations.

Sliti disclosed in an interview that he turned down an offer from La Liga team RCD Mallorca before joining Saudi side Al-Ettifaq. He said that he turned down an offer from Real Mallorca because he wished to remain near the holy Kaaba and raise his children according to Islamic teachings.

Net Worth

His current net worth is believed to be over $2 million as of December 2023, which he has accumulated from his football career.
