How much does a wedding DJ cost?

EVERY COUPLE wants to have a wedding that not only they will remember, but their guests won't forget.

With that comes excellent entertainment to keep people on their feet.

How much does a wedding DJ cost?

For couples to make their wedding a night to remember, a DJ could offer a wide variety of music in different styles.

According to reports, newlywed couples can spend anywhere from $600 to $3,500 on a DJ.

Experienced DJs can read a crowd, keep people on the dance floor, and tend to have better equipment for music customization.

It is important to note that these DJs usually do not have the same experience and customizability as higher-priced DJ.

How do DJs set their prices?

DJs usually set their prices depending on the city where the wedding will be located, travel expenses, the DJ's experience level, and the DJ's reputation.

Some DJs present couples with one nightly rate, while others bill by the hour.

What questions should I ask a DJ before hiring?

When you are hiring a DJ, it is always a good idea to get to know them a little.

Here is a list of questions to ask your potential wedding DJ to make sure that he or she is a good fit you, your partner, and your wedding.

  • What types of music do you prefer playing?
  • How do you typically work with couples to create a wedding playlist?
  • How long will you play for? Is there a minimum number of hours you'll work?
  • What is your rate and is gratuity included?
  • What equipment do you include, and what services are additional?
  • When will you arrive to set up?
  • What happens if you get sick or have an emergency and can't make it to our wedding?
  • Are you insured?
