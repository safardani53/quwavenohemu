The Voice stage invader wants Emma Willis to apologise to HIM for treating him 'like a criminal'

THE Voice stage invader Dr AC says host Emma Willis should apologise to HIM for the way he was treated by show bosses.

The London-based DJ and philosopher - real name Konstantin - broke his silence in an interview with The Sun's Bizarre Editor Dan Wootton who had earlier branded him an "idiot" on ITV's Lorraine.

He said: "Emma Willis, she should apologise for treating me like a criminal for wanting to express ideas.

"She left me in the hands of the security who literally chopped me to death. What if I had died? What would she have said?

"Emma Willis doesn't know the meaning of philosophy or a philosopher. She treated me like a criminal for holding up ideas. How can she be so insensitive?"

Konstantin caused chaos during the live final of The Voice on Sunday by storming the stage as the runner-up results were revealed by host Emma.

The Sun's Bizarre column earlier exclusively revealed his identity and his claims that he was nearly KILLED by ITV security.

He was pounced on by security guards as he held up a sign which read: "I demand change in the way big media control information based on four ideas: positive thinking, movement, creativity and fairness."

The wannabe rapper phoned up Bizarre's Dan Wootton to vent his anger after he called him an "idiot" and ITV's Lorraine branded him a "buffoon".

The prankster lives in North West London but claims he was born in "Mars".

Asked where he is originally from, he replied: "I am from Mars, from a different planet. From the planet of creativity and ideas. I was born in Mars."

MOST POPULAR

Despite interrupting the talent show final, The Sun's Bizarre column can reveal that Konstantin auditioned for Britain's Got Talent five years ago as a wannabe rapper - but failed to make it through.

He admitted: "I tried Britain's Got Talent, I was trying to rap a few ideas around five years ago, but never again. Britain's Got Talent is based on people that don't have talent."

Konstantin has also performed live on BBC Three for Radio 1 breakfast host Nick Grimshaw's panel show Sweat The Small Stuff.

His stunt live on ITV's The Voice is not the first time he has attempted to invade media organisations.

The activist claims he has previously been arrested for similar efforts at Fox, the BBC and the New York Times.

He said: "I have spent ten years trying to express my ideas. I was arrested in New York city by Fox TV, no one spoke about it. I was arrested by the BBC for trying to express my ideas, no one spoke about it. I was arrested at the New York Times, no one spoke about it."

The self-professed philosopher also dismissed claims that his stage invasion could have been mistaken for an attempted terror attack in light of recent events.

He blasted: "I was holding up ideas, not attacking anyone, not resisting any order. A man with a gun is totally different from a man with ideas."

After being released last night Konstantin took to social media to vent his fury, accusing security guards of almost killing him and branding ITV bosses "Nazis".

He tweeted: "The Security of @ITV almost choked me to death for wantin to show a few ideas – Big Media praises security! #TheVoiceUK,

“#CreativeismMovement The #EVIL #Nazis of @ITV must have learnt from #Hitler the way they murder independent thinkers. #TheVoiceUK #Einstein.

“#CreativeismMovement I will report tomorrow to the corrupted @metpoliceuk Because I was choked to death for showing a few ideas #TheVoiceUK.”

Fans were left bewildered when he leapt onto the stage holding a piece of paper showing a message, but viewers unable to figure out what was written.

He later shared a picture of the sign in progress as he designed it on his PC.

It read: "Journalists of the big media or middle-age and ancient priests; Stop murdering independent thinking.

"Oppression of ideas is not freedom. Thug-life is not art.

"War is not peace.

"I demand change in the way big media control information based on four ideas.

"Positive thinking. Movement creativity. And fairness."

Konstantin is also an author under his nickname Dr AC.

He recently penned the e-book Ten Original Qualities That One Needs to Change The World: Art, Ideas and Creativity.

It is yet to be reviewed on Amazon, but according to the author, the tome "intends to show what one needs in order to change the world.

"In this Ebook I show the right way to the world. And I do that by explaining ten original qualities of the beings of being which I think are embedded deep in the heart and soul of humanity.

"So in this Ebook you will find the answers of questions like 'How can one be a creator? What does one need to lead, bring change and create?'"

Police were not called to the TV studios last night and Konstantin was escorted off the premises by security staff.

A statement from ITV read: "During the results this evening there was a brief interruption on stage, which was dealt with immediately by security. The show continued as planned.”

The Voice was won by Mo Adeniran who beat Welsh duo Into The Ark and singer Jamie Miller to the prize of a record deal with Polydor.
