Cedi Osman: Basketball Career

As previously mentioned, Osman started playing basketball at a very young age. In 2001, he started playing basketball with KK Bosna’s youth team. However, his professional basketball career began in 2007.

Professional Career

After his incredible performance at KK Bosna, he signed a youth team contract with Anadolu Efes.

Moreover, Osman was loaned out for the 2011-12 season to Pertevniyal of the Turkish Basketball Premier League. Later in 2012, he signed a  professional contract with the Anadolu Efes senior team.

As of now, he is playing for the Cleveland Cavaliers as a small forward. His journey with Cleveland is very long.  

In 2018, Osman contributed 16 points, six rebounds, and five assists in a 38 minutes game with Atlanta Hawks. The Cavaliers reached the 2018 NBA Finals but lost 40 to the Golden State Warriors.

Cedi scored a career-high 29 points on January 25, 2019, in a 100-94 loss to the Miami Heat. A few days later, he was named a participant in the Rising Stars Challenge 2019 as a member of the World Team.

Osman signed an extension contract with the Cavaliers on 26th October 2019.

National Career and Achievements

Cedi Osman played the 2011 FIBA Europe Under-16 Championship and the 2012 FIBA Europe Under-18 Championship. He was named the Most Talented Player and won a bronze medal in the 2012 Albert Schweitzer Tournament.

Further, he also won a gold medal at the 2013 FIBA Europe Under-18 and 2014 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship. 

He became a member of the senior men’s Turkish national basketball team after his extraordinary performance in the junior team. 

Osman has played FIBA Basketball World Cup 2014, 2015 Euro Basket, and the 2016 Manila FIBA World Olympic Qualifying Tournament with the senior national team.
