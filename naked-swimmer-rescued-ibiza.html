Drone captures the moment four men save a nude swimmer's life by pulling him from the sea off Ibiza

DRONE footage has emerged showing the dramatic moment four men rescued a naked swimmer struggling to stay afloat in rough waters off the coast of Ibiza.

In the clip posted on video-sharing site Newsflare, a man can be seen caught up in rip tide while swimming in the Mediterranean Sea.

Photographer Eric Snyder, from Boston, Massachusetts, was in the Spanish party island on a one-week trip with other snappers and models when the scene unfolded.

He was filmed with three others clambering down a rocky cliff after spotting the struggling swimmer.

They managed to haul the swimmer to safety before realising he had no clothes on.

The video shows the nude swimmer giving the camera a sheepish wave when he realises the whole rescue was captured.

Recalling the dramatic rescue, Eric said: "I was standing about 100 yards from the ocean just watching all the other models and photographers, and I looked out and saw this guy just swimming, bobbing up and down.

"I looked at him and gave him a thumbs up twice - he looked like he was having a hard time swimming."

After hearing the man shout for help twice, Eric said he gathered a group of men before rushing down the shore on the rocks.

He added: "I was signalling the guy to swim across the shore and not towards it, the rip currents was extremely strong!

"I had a small towel in my hand that I used to reach out to him and hold on to the rocks.

"I grabbed his hand and it slipped the first time and he fell back into the water, and the waves pushed him up close to me again and that's when I grabbed him and pulled him up onto the flat rock - and that's when we discovered he was naked."

The man was reportedly unharmed by the ordeal.
