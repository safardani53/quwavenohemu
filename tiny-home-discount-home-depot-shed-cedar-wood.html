Home Depot discounts tiny home by $405 to $5,394 and it comes with long-lasting use and a work

HOME Depot has provided its customers with a sizable discount on a rustic and rugged shed.

This 64-square-foot cedar structure which could easily be converted into a tiny home usually costs $5,799 plus shipping.

But Home Depot is now selling the structure for just $5,394, according to the company's website.

The product description notes that the building's red cedar construction makes it particularly durable.

"Its natural resistance to decay and insects ensures long-lasting use and enjoyment," it says.

The home has a vintage old-world vibe that would make it the perfect base for a secluded cottage in the woods.

Although the shed is relatively bare bones, it comes with a workbench and decorative flower boxes that sit below each window.

The product is shipped in pieces and needs to be assembled by the buyer when it arrives.

One customer said it took about two days to build with two people working on it.

Another person who bought the shed said construction took five days, but this was because they poured a concrete pad for it to sit on first.
