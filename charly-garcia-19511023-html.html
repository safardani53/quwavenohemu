Charly García is an Argentinian singer, songwriter, songwriter and record producer. With a vast and famous career, he has formed and directed two of the most popular groups in the history of Argentine rock: Sui Generis in the 1970s and Serú Girán in the 1980s, as well as cult groups such as the singer of progressive rock Hacer Pájaros' La Máquina and super folk rock group PorSuiGieco, both also in the 1970s. Since the 1980s García has operated primarily as a solo musician. His main instruments are the piano, then the guitar and the keyboard. García is critically regarded as one of South America's most influential rock artists and the "father of Argentinian rock". García is also famous for her two-tone mustache, white on one side due to vitiligo.
