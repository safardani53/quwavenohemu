North and Saint West's jaw-dropping movie salary demands revealed after siblings make film debut in

KIM Kardashian's children North and Saint West's salaries for their role in the new Paw Patrol movie have been revealed.

North, 10, and Saint, seven, lent their voices to Paw Patrol: The Mighty Movie, which came out last month.

North earned $5,000 per recording session while playing the role of Mini, the Pomeranian Junior Patroller, according to documents obtained by TMZ.

The preteen was guaranteed four recording sessions, meaning she raked in a whopping $20,000 from the role.

Saint was also given $5,000 for each session for his portrayal of Meteor Max.

He had two recording sessions, meaning he walked away with $10,000.

Kim also appeared in the cartoon children's movie, reprising her role as Delores the poodle.

The Paw Patrol sequel follows the pups, who are given magical new powers after an asteroid lands in Adventure City.

The voice cast includes James Marsden, McKenna Grace, Kristen Bell, Marsai Martin, Serena Williams, Taraji P. Henson, Lil Rel Howrey and Chris Rock.

Kim shares her four children - North, Saint, Chicago, five, and Psalm, three - with her ex-husband Kanye West.

Her kids are no stranger to money-making deals.

In March, The U.S. Sun exclusively revealed that North is set to launch skincare and toy lines.

Kim - who has a $1billion business empire - has filed four trademarks in her daughter's name.

The first trademark filed is for "non-medicated skin preparations, skin moisturizers, skin lotions, skin creams, skin cleansers."

North's beauty line also plans to expand into: "Skin serums, facial oils, body oils, bath and shower gels, bubble bath, body powders, cosmetics, fragrances, hair care preparations," and more.

NORTH'S EMPIRE

The Hulu star also plans for North to build her own toy business empire, as a second trademark details "toy figures, doll accessories, dolls, drawing toys, infant toys, mechanical action toys, play sets for action figures, soft sculpture toys, bath toys," and more.

Her toy company may also include: "Children's educational toys for developing fine motor and cognitive skills, musical toys, toy food, toy cookware, baby gyms, playground balls, and sport balls."

North's third trademark promises advertising services and her fourth includes "entertainment in the nature of providing information in means of a global computer network in the fields of entertainment and pop culture."

Kim filed similar trademarks for North back in 2019, but at the time included an additional trademark for clothing.

Back in November 2022, Kim also filed trademarks for Psalm for skincare, supplements, diapers, and strollers, among other items.
