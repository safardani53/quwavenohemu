Brittany Matthews | Early Life, Family, And Education

Brittany was born on September 1, 1995, in Whitehouse, Texas, to Martin Scott Matthews and Diana Massey. However, her parents divorced when Britt was very young.

The ex-couple maintains a cordial relationship for the sake of their children. Both exes are happily married in separate relationships as of now.

Her step-father, Paul Massey, collapsed while waiting to enter Arrowhead Stadium for a Chief game in 2018. Sadly, he was unable to recover and passed away. 

Besides, she has two brothers, Devin Matthews and half-brother Landon Massey.

Brittnay has been an athlete and active sport all her life. She attended the same high school as her future husband, i.e., Whitehouse High School. In high school, she was a member of the cheerleading and soccer teams.

Furthermore, she also continued her love for soccer in college. The American trainer attended the University of Texas, unlike her fiancé, who attended Texas Tech University.

Later, she graduated with a Bachelor of Science degree in Kinesiology.

Brittany Matthews | Profession, Job, And Soccer Career

School And College-Level Soccer

The famous trainer went to Whitehouse High School, where she played soccer on the school team. Initially, Brittany was unsure if she wanted to be a part of collegiate-level soccer but later on, seeing the University Of Texas’s team, she became enthusiastic.

The UT Tyler Patriot assisted her team in the ASC Academic All-Conference Team. She was a two-time recipient of the American Southwest Conference’s Offensive Player of the Week.

Not to mention, Brittany is second all-time in program history with 78 points and received All-Region honors after her senior season.

Additionally, she scored four hattricks in wins against McMurry and Sul Ross consecutively and over Belhaven and Louisiana College, which set a new record.

In her career at UT, she made 31 goals and 16 assists. Furthermore, the forward player-led Patriots to a spectacular record of 56-13-5.

Her exceptional soccer career with the Patriots inspired her to pursue a professional career.

Professional Soccer

Due to her incredible skills, she signed a contract with the Icelandic multi-sports club UMF Afturelding. She only spent one season in Europe; however, that was all she needed to win her first championship trophy. 

The UMFA’s interest rose after she established a new UT Tyler single-season record with 18 goals. Her husband, family, and coaches were in the room when she made the deal.

After graduation, she went to Iceland for the summer while her boyfriend was in the Chiefs. The forward player assisted the team for a few seasons before pursuing a fitness and training career.

Fitness And Business

Following an excellent professional career, she became an experienced fitness trainer.

She now inspires people to achieve their fitness targets through her website. Besides that, she also runs a fitness company with paid programs to assist people in attaining their best body. 

Similarly, she signed an exclusive deal with an apparel Company named Balance Athletica. The QB’s wife is also the co-owner of KC NWSL, a professional women’s soccer team.
